# If Penguins Could Fly — Lachlan Edition

## Visual world

Bright polar storybook adventure: chunky silhouettes, glossy painted shading, bold midnight outlines, and playful aviation machinery. The penguin and blue-orange plane remain the visual anchor.

## Palette

- Glacier sky: `#67D6FF`
- Midnight navy: `#102A56`
- Signal orange: `#FF7A45`
- Aurora gold: `#FFD34D`
- Snow white: `#F6FBFF`
- Storm violet: `#6C5B8F`

## Signature

The opening story and tutorial are presented as Lachlan's illustrated flight log. Hand-drawn route marks, stamps, and short radio messages carry the player from the Antarctic coast into increasingly impossible skies.

## Rules

- No emoji as finished game art.
- Every important object needs a strong silhouette at phone size.
- Motion should communicate state: climbing, diving, danger, protection, and success.
- Each world changes geography, atmosphere, particles, and color—not just a tint.

## Progression

- Perfect center passes build a visible streak and reward precision.
- Flight missions rotate through rings, fish, rescues, map fragments, and perfect passes.
- Completed missions award persistent flight badges used to unlock four scarf, glow, and trail kits.
- The route map always shows a real journey: Coast → Sunset → Aurora → Peaks → Space.
- Boss fights stay one-button friendly: collect ice fish, then tap to fire while climbing.
- Boss escalation: Battle Cruiser → cropped Dreadnought Gun Deck → the full, oversized Dreadnought finale.
- The Storm Run after boss one is deliberately long and hectic, but its horizontal speed is capped for readability.

## Boss artwork

- Boss one descends on a dedicated articulated brass gantry with hydraulic joints, chains, and a heavy clamp.
- Boss two has its own detachable gun-deck sprite with a visible docking collar; it is no longer a crop of the final ship.
